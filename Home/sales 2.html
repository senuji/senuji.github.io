Mint Green Tea

Made by combining natural spearmint and peppermint flavors with Ceylon Green Tea, mint green tea carries a more lively and vibrant flavor that soothes senses and cools down the body whether consumed hot or cold. Mint green tea is manufactured in Sri Lanka only includes natural mint flavoring and premium quality Ceylon Green Teas assuring the combined goodness of green tea and mint.

Ranawara Tea

Ranawara or Cassia auriculata is a popular herb among Sri Lankans. Ranawara plants are mostly available in the coastal line and the dry zones in Sri Lanka. The herbal tea made with the flower of this plant has been widely used by Sri Lankans for generations. It is prepared by boiling Ranawara flowers in hot water.

Jasmine Green Tea

Subtly sweet and highly fragrant, jasmine green tea is prepared mainly by mixing tea leaves with freshly bloomed jasmine blooms for over four hours. Stored under controlled humidity, tea leaves are dried again to remove the moisture gained during the scenting process. Although green tea is the most popular based for jasmine tea, it is also prepared with white oolong tea. Several green tea manufacturers in Sri Lanka specializing in the production of Jasmine Green Tea with Ceylon Green Tea

Moringa Tea

The leaves of Moringa, the drumstick tree, are boiled to make moringa herbal tea, a refreshing healthy beverage that supports the body in many ways. The moringa tree is also known as the “Miracle Tree” because of its amazing values. Moringa Tea is made by steeping dried moringa leaves in freshly boiled spring water for about five minutes before being strained.

Gotukola Tea

Gotu-Kola or Centella asiatica is a herb that belongs to the Parsley family. It is also known as the “herb of longevity”. Gotu-kola sambol is a popular dish in Sri Lankan cuisine. The plant grows in warm, tropical climates. Most Sri Lankans consume Gotu-Kola to receive the numerous health benefits the plant has to offer.
